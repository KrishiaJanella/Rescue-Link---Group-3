Philippines scrambles to contain oil spill and locate sunken fuel tanker

MANILA, March 3 (Reuters) - Environment and disaster authorities in the Philippines rushed to contain an oil spill on Friday from a sunken fuel tanker that has reached coastal towns on a large central island, warning of dangers to marine ecosystems if more oil leaks. The tanker, MT Princess Empress, was still missing on Friday after sinking en route to Iloilo province carrying about 800,000 litres (211,338 gallons) of industrial fuel oil.

Sunken Philippine oil tanker found after 3 weeks as spill spreads
