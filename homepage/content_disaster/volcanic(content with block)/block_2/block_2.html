Preparing for a Volcanic Eruption: Essential Steps for Safety

      Living in proximity to a volcano presents unique challenges, requiring proactive measures to ensure safety in the event of an eruption. Understanding the dynamics of volcanic activity, emergency preparedness, staying informed, evacuation procedures, and protective measures are paramount. In this guide, we'll delve into these five crucial topics to equip you with the knowledge and tools necessary to react effectively when faced with the threat of a volcanic eruption.





Understanding Volcanic Activity

Before preparing for an eruption, it's crucial to understand the nature of volcanic activity. Volcanoes can erupt suddenly or show signs of unrest for weeks or months before an eruption. Monitoring agencies use various instruments to track changes in seismic activity, gas emissions, and ground deformation to assess the level of risk.




Emergency Preparedness

Developing an emergency plan is essential for individuals living in volcanic hazard zones. This plan should include evacuation routes, communication strategies, and designated meeting points. Families should also prepare emergency kits with essential supplies such as water, food, first aid supplies, and important documents.




Stay Informed

Keeping abreast of the latest information from local authorities and volcanic monitoring agencies is crucial. Authorities typically issue alerts and warnings based on the level of volcanic activity. It's important to have multiple sources of information, including radio, television, social media, and mobile alerts.




Evacuation Procedures

Knowing when and how to evacuate safely is vital during a volcanic eruption. Authorities may issue evacuation orders based on the level of risk and the direction of volcanic hazards such as lava flows, pyroclastic flows, and ashfall. Following evacuation orders promptly and knowing evacuation routes can save lives.




Protective Measures

During an eruption, volcanic hazards such as ashfall, toxic gases, and lahars pose significant risks to health and safety. It's essential to take protective measures such as staying indoors, wearing masks to filter ash particles, and avoiding areas prone to lahars. Following advisories from health authorities can help minimize exposure to volcanic hazards.

Watch video on What to do BEFORE, DURING and AFTER a VOLCANIC ERUPTION?

This is a set of life-saving tips and guidelines for people living in areas prone to volcanic eruptions, specifically in provinces like Batangas or Bicol in the Philippines. The tips are categorized into three sections: before, during, and after a volcanic eruption. Before a volcanic eruption, it is crucial to be aware of the danger zone, have a 'go bag' ready with essentials, know the evacuation plan, coordinate with relatives or barangay officials for transportation, and understand the alert levels and history of the volcano. During a volcanic eruption, the only safe option is to evacuate the area immediately. If ash fall occurs, it is recommended to close doors and windows, cover them with wet cloth, properly cover food and water, stay indoors, use eye goggles and masks, and avoid ingesting ash. After a volcanic eruption, it is important not to return home without the go-ahead from authorities, see a doctor if necessary, have the house checked for safety, and follow proper guidelines when cleaning up after an ash fall. These tips aim to help people living near volcanoes be better prepared and informed in case of a volcanic eruption.
