Earthquake

An earthquake is a natural phenomenon characterized by the shaking and trembling of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. It can range in intensity from barely noticeable to devastating, resulting in destruction of buildings, infrastructure, and loss of life. Earthquakes occur when there is a release of energy stored in the Earth's crust, often along fault lines. They can trigger secondary hazards like landslides, tsunamis, and aftershocks, making them one of the most unpredictable and destructive natural disasters. Seismologists use instruments called seismographs to measure and study earthquakes, helping to understand their causes and mitigate their impact.

Stay safe during an Earthquake

During an earthquake, remember to "Drop, Cover, and Hold On." Stay indoors if inside, move to an open area if outside, and stay inside your vehicle if driving. Watch out for aftershocks and hazards afterward.

Learn More

Stay safe after an Earthquake

After an earthquake, prioritize safety by checking for hazards like gas leaks and damaged utilities. Be cautious of aftershocks and avoid damaged buildings. If necessary, evacuate to a safe location and listen to local authorities for updates and instructions.
