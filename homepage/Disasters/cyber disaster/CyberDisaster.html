CYBER DISASTER

A cyber disaster refers to a significant event or series of events in the digital realm that result in widespread disruption, damage, or loss. These disasters can take many forms, including large-scale cyberattacks, data breaches, infrastructure failures, or the compromise of critical systems. Cyber disasters often have severe consequences for individuals, organizations, or even entire nations, impacting everything from financial stability to national security. Examples include ransomware attacks that paralyze businesses, data breaches that expose sensitive information, or attacks on critical infrastructure like power grids or transportation systems. Cyber disasters highlight the vulnerabilities inherent in our increasingly interconnected digital world and underscore the importance of robust cybersecurity measures to prevent and mitigate their effects.
