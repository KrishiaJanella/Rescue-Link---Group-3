Flood

Floods are natural disasters that occur when water covers land that is usually dry. This typically happens when there is an overflow of water onto land that is normally dry, which can be caused by heavy rain, melting snow, river overflow, storm surges, or dam failures. Floods can happen gradually or suddenly, and they can cause significant damage to homes, infrastructure, agriculture, and the environment. They are one of the most common and widespread natural hazards, affecting communities worldwide. Floods can have devastating impacts on people's lives, leading to displacement, loss of life, and economic hardship.

Prepare for a Food

Prepare for food shortages during a flood by stocking up on canned goods, rice, pasta, and bottled water. Keep a manual can opener, portable stove, and cooking utensils handy. Store emergency food supplies in a waterproof container in a high location. Stay updated on flood warnings and evacuation orders for safety.

Floodwater Safety

During floods, it's important to avoid touching floodwater due to contamination risks. Wear protective gear like rubber boots and gloves if you must enter the water. Always wash hands with clean water and soap after contact. Use bottled or boiled water for drinking and cooking, discard any food that touched floodwater, and be cautious of debris and electrical hazards.

Returning Home

Returning home after a flood? Check for structural damage and electrical hazards before entering. Wear protective gear like rubber boots and gloves, and discard any contaminated items. Follow cleaning protocols to prevent mold growth and monitor your health for any symptoms related to flood exposure.
